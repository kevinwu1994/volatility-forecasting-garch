## What This Presentation Demonstrates

The slides walk through a complete end-to-end quantitative workflow:

- Identification of stylized facts in financial returns  
- Specification and estimation of GARCH-family models  
- Out-of-sample volatility forecasting using a rolling framework  
- Model evaluation with MSE, MAE, and QLIKE loss functions  
- Transformation of volatility forecasts into Value-at-Risk (VaR)  
- Statistical validation using Kupiec backtesting  
- Interpretation of results for real-world risk management  

---

## Key Findings

- The standard **GARCH(1,1)** model consistently outperforms more complex alternatives in out-of-sample forecasting  
- Increased model complexity does not necessarily improve predictive accuracy  
- Volatility forecasts can be effectively translated into **well-calibrated VaR estimates**  

---

## Practical Insight

This project highlights a key principle in quantitative modeling:

> **Robustness and generalization are often more valuable than model complexity in real-world financial applications.**

---

## Key Takeaway

A parsimonious GARCH(1,1) model delivers stable volatility forecasts and produces reliable, statistically validated risk measures suitable for practical implementation.
